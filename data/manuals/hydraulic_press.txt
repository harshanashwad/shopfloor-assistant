HYDRAULIC PRESS HP-200 — OPERATION AND FAULT REFERENCE

Overview
The HP-200 is a 200-ton hydraulic press used for sheet metal forming operations. It is
controlled via the Siemens HMI panel mounted on the front guarding. Normal operating
pressure is 180-210 bar. Cycle time under standard load is 8-12 seconds.

Startup Procedure
1. Confirm the area is clear and light curtains are active (green indicator on HMI).
2. Turn the main disconnect to ON and wait for the hydraulic pump to reach idle pressure.
3. Home the ram by pressing HOME on the HMI. Do not attempt manual jog before homing.
4. Load the die set and verify clamp pressure reads above 150 bar before running cycles.

Common Alarms

ALM-101: Low Hydraulic Pressure
Cause: reservoir level low, or pump cavitation. Check sight glass on the tank; top up
with ISO 46 hydraulic oil if below the minimum line. If oil level is normal, check the
suction filter for clogging.

ALM-114: Ram Position Fault
Cause: the ram did not reach the commanded position within the timeout window. Usually
caused by a worn position sensor or an obstruction in the die area. Clear the area, re-home
the ram, and re-run. If the fault repeats twice, escalate to maintenance — this can
indicate sensor drift requiring recalibration.

ALM-130: Overpressure Trip
Cause: pressure exceeded 230 bar, safety relief triggered. Do not reset without inspecting
the die for misalignment or double-blank feeding. This is a hard stop and should not be
bypassed by operators.

ALM-142: E-Stop Circuit Open
Cause: an e-stop button is engaged or the circuit has a wiring fault. Check all e-stops
around the cell are released. If all are released and the fault persists, this requires
electrical maintenance — do not attempt to jumper the circuit.

Routine Maintenance Notes
Daily: check oil level, inspect hoses for wear, verify light curtain function.
Weekly: clean suction filter, check die clamp torque.
Any hose showing bulging or weeping oil must be reported immediately and the press
tagged out until replaced.

Escalation Guidance
Operators are expected to handle ALM-101 and single occurrences of ALM-114 independently.
ALM-130 and ALM-142 should always be escalated to maintenance regardless of operator
experience level, as they involve safety-critical subsystems.
