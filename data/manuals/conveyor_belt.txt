CONVEYOR BELT SYSTEM CB-12 — OPERATION AND FAULT REFERENCE

Overview
CB-12 is the main line conveyor feeding parts from the press cell to the packing station.
It runs at a fixed 0.4 m/s belt speed with three zone motors and photo-eye sensors at each
transfer point. Control is via a local panel with status lights, tied into the line PLC.

Startup Procedure
1. Confirm upstream and downstream stations report ready on the line status board.
2. Press RESET on the local panel to clear any latched faults from the previous shift.
3. Press START and observe all three zone motors engage in sequence within 3 seconds.
4. Confirm parts transfer cleanly across each photo-eye without stalling.

Common Alarms

ALM-201: Photo-Eye Blocked
Cause: a part has stalled at a transfer point, or the sensor lens is dirty. Clear any
stalled part by hand (belt stopped) and wipe the sensor lens. This is the most frequent
fault and is fully operator-correctable.

ALM-208: Zone Motor Overload
Cause: excessive load on a zone motor, often from part jam upstream of the zone or a
seized roller. Inspect the zone for jammed material. If rollers feel stiff when turned
by hand, escalate for bearing inspection.

ALM-215: Belt Tracking Fault
Cause: belt has drifted off-center, tripping the tracking sensor. Minor drift can be
corrected by adjusting the tensioner bolts per the marked reference points. Significant
or repeated drift indicates roller misalignment and should be escalated.

ALM-222: E-Stop / Safety Gate Open
Cause: a safety gate along the line is open or an e-stop is engaged. Verify all gates are
closed and latched. If the fault persists with gates closed, this is a safety-circuit
issue and must be escalated — do not bypass the interlock.

ALM-230: Communication Loss with Line PLC
Cause: network or PLC fault upstream of the local panel. Not operator-serviceable;
escalate to controls/maintenance immediately, as this can affect the whole line.

Routine Maintenance Notes
Daily: wipe photo-eye lenses, visually check belt tracking and tension.
Weekly: check roller bearings for noise or play, inspect drive chain tension.

Escalation Guidance
ALM-201 is fully operator-correctable and should not be escalated on its own. ALM-208 and
ALM-215 are correctable on first occurrence with escalation only if recurring. ALM-222 and
ALM-230 require immediate escalation regardless of operator confidence level.
